AfuChat gives you granular control over who sees your content and how others can interact with you. All privacy settings take effect immediately and are synced to your account, so they apply across every device you use. You can find them under **Settings → Privacy**.

## Profile visibility — public vs. private account

By default your AfuChat account is **public**: anyone on the platform can view your posts, stories, and profile information. Switching to a **private account** limits your content to approved followers only.

To toggle private mode:

1. Go to **Settings → Privacy → Account Privacy**.
2. Turn on **Private Account**.

When private mode is active, new follow requests must be approved by you before the requester can see your posts or stories. Existing followers are not affected — they keep access unless you remove them.

You can also control whether your **bio is visible publicly** (to non-followers) separately from the private account toggle.

<Note>
  Switching to a private account does not retroactively hide content you previously shared publicly. Posts and stories that were visible before you enabled private mode may remain accessible through direct links until they naturally expire or you delete them.
</Note>

## Who can message you

Control which users can start new conversations with you under **Settings → Privacy → Messages**.

| Option | Who can send you messages |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Any AfuChat user — the default setting |
| **Followers Only** | Only people you mutually follow |
| **Nobody** | Turns off incoming messages from anyone new |

This setting affects new conversation requests only. Messages within existing chats are not affected by changes to this setting.

## Who can see your stories

Story visibility is governed by your overall account privacy:

- **Public account** — your stories are visible to all AfuChat users.
- **Private account** — only approved followers can view your stories.

You can additionally mute specific users from seeing your stories from within the story composer or from a user's profile page, without needing to block them.

## Last seen and online status

Under **Settings → Privacy → Account Privacy**, two toggles let you control your activity indicators:

- **Show Online Status** — when enabled, a green dot appears next to your name in chats while you are actively using the app.
- **Show Last Seen** — when enabled, your contacts can see the approximate time you were last active.

Turning off either toggle hides that information from all other users. Note that if you disable **Show Last Seen**, you will also not be able to see other users' last-seen timestamps — this is a mutual setting.

## Visibility of followers and following lists

Under **Settings → Privacy → Visibility** you can independently hide your followers list and your following list from other users:

- **Hide Followers List** — others cannot see who follows you.
- **Hide Following List** — others cannot see who you follow.

You can also limit your posts so they don't appear in Discover for non-followers, and opt out of search results entirely with **Hide From Search**.

## Blocked users

Blocking a user prevents them from seeing your profile, sending you messages, or interacting with your posts and stories. They are not notified when you block them.

To block someone: go to their profile, tap the three-dot menu, and select **Block**. To view and manage your blocked list, go to **Settings → Privacy → Blocked Users**.

Unblocking a user restores their ability to find your profile and send follow requests, but does not automatically restore any previous follow relationship.

## Restricted accounts

**Restricted accounts** offer a lighter alternative to blocking. When you restrict someone:

- Their comments on your posts are only visible to them (not to your other followers) until you approve them.
- They cannot see when you are online or when you've read their messages.
- They can still follow you if your account is public.

Manage restricted accounts under **Settings → Privacy → Restricted Accounts**.

## Download your data

You can request a full export of your AfuChat data — including your profile, posts, messages, activity history, and transaction records — at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security settings">
    Go to **Settings → Security & Data** and tap **Download My Data**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify your identity">
    If 2FA is active, enter your current authenticator code to confirm the request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your export">
    AfuChat processes the export and sends a JSON file to your registered email address. Check your inbox — the email typically arrives within a few minutes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Delete your account

Account deletion is a permanent action. AfuChat places deleted accounts in a **30-day grace period** before all data is erased — you can log back in and restore your account at any time during that window. After 30 days, your account, posts, messages, and purchases are permanently removed with no possibility of recovery.

To request deletion, go to **Settings → Security & Data → Account Closure**. You'll be asked to confirm a deletion reason, re-enter your email address, and type your @handle before the final action is available. Two-factor authentication is required if it's enabled on your account.

If you change your mind after initiating deletion, sign in before the 30-day window closes — you'll be prompted to restore your account.
