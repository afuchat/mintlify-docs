AfuChat gives you multiple layers of account protection — from a strong password and two-factor authentication to biometric device lock. Spending a few minutes in **Settings → Security & Data** significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access.

## Change your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Security settings">
    Go to **Settings → Security & Data** and tap **Change Password**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pass the 2FA check">
    If two-factor authentication is active on your account, AfuChat will ask you to enter your current authenticator code before proceeding. This step protects you even if someone else has physical access to your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter and confirm your new password">
    Type your new password (minimum 6 characters) and confirm it in the second field. Tap **Update Password** to save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After a successful password change you remain signed in on your current device. Other active sessions are not automatically revoked, so if you believe your account has been compromised, sign out from all devices and update your password immediately.

## Two-factor authentication (2FA)

AfuChat supports **TOTP-based 2FA** (Time-based One-Time Password) compatible with Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, and any RFC 6238–compliant app. Once enabled, sensitive account actions — such as changing your password or exporting your data — require a fresh authenticator code in addition to your password.

### Enable 2FA

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the 2FA screen">
    In **Settings → Security & Data**, tap **Enable 2FA** (or **Manage 2FA** if it's already on).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add AfuChat to your authenticator app">
    Open your authenticator app, tap **+** or **Add account**, choose **Scan a QR code**, and scan the QR shown on screen. If you can't scan the QR, tap **Can't scan? Show manual key** to reveal the text secret and enter it manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm you see a rotating code">
    Tick the confirmation checkbox once your authenticator shows an **AfuChat** entry with a 6-digit rotating code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the current code to verify">
    Type the 6-digit code currently shown in your authenticator app. AfuChat auto-submits when all six digits are entered. Codes refresh every 30 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once verified, your account security score updates to **Strong** and the 2FA card in Security settings turns green.

### Disable 2FA

Tap **Disable Two-Factor Auth** on the 2FA screen. You must enter a valid authenticator code to confirm the change — this prevents someone from turning off 2FA without your authenticator device.

### Recovery options

If you lose access to your authenticator app, you can remove 2FA via email recovery:

1. On the 2FA screen, tap **Reset via Email**.
2. Enter your registered email address and tap **Send Recovery Code**.
3. Enter the 6-digit code sent to your inbox.
4. AfuChat verifies the code and removes 2FA from your account.

<Warning>
  AfuChat does not issue backup codes. Your email address is the only recovery path if you lose your authenticator app. Make sure your registered email is one you can always access — and keep your authenticator app backed up (Authy and 1Password both offer multi-device sync). If you permanently lose access to both your authenticator and your email, account recovery may not be possible.
</Warning>

## Device security

Under the **Device Security** row in Security settings you can configure:

- **PIN lock** — require a numeric PIN to open the app
- **Biometric unlock** — Face ID or Touch ID (set up automatically after your first password login on a supported device)

Biometric sign-in lets you unlock the app instantly without re-entering your password. If biometric authentication is no longer available on a device (for example, after a factory reset), you will be prompted to sign in with your password to re-enable it.

## Sign out of this device

Signing out from **Settings → Security & Data → Sign Out of This Device** permanently erases all local data — cached messages, downloaded media, and local settings — from that device. Your account and all cloud-stored data remain intact. You will need your password (or biometric, if set up) to sign back in.
