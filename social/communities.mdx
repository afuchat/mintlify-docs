Communities bring together two powerful ways to connect with people on AfuChat: **Groups** for real-time two-way conversation among members, and **Channels** for broadcasting content to a wider audience of subscribers. Both live in the Communities tab, making it easy to find relevant spaces and build your own.

## What Communities Are

AfuChat organises community spaces into two distinct types:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Groups" icon="users">
    A public group is a shared chat room where every member can send messages, share media, and react to what others post. Groups display a member count and are discoverable by anyone on AfuChat. They work best for discussions, support circles, hobbyist clubs, and local meetups.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Channels" icon="megaphone">
    A channel is a one-to-many broadcast space. The owner (and admins they designate) publish posts that all subscribers can read and react to. Channels are ranked by subscriber count, verified channels display a badge, and each channel shows its owner's @handle.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Finding and Joining Communities

Open the **Communities** tab from the bottom navigation bar. You'll see two sub-tabs: **Groups** and **Channels**.

- **Groups** are listed in order of most recently active.
- **Channels** are listed in order of subscriber count, so the most popular channels appear first.

Each card shows the community name, description, and a member or subscriber count. To join:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse or scroll">
    Scroll through the list to find a community that interests you. Pull down to refresh and see newly created communities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Join or Follow">
    On a group card, tap **Join** to become a member instantly. On a channel card, tap **Follow** to subscribe. Both actions require an internet connection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the community">
    After joining, the button changes to **Open** and you are taken directly into the group chat or channel feed. The community will also appear in your Chats list for quick access later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating Your Own Community

Tap the **New Group** or **New Channel** button in the top-right corner of the Communities tab to start building your own space.

### Creating a Group

Give your group a name, write a short description, and upload a cover image. To make your group discoverable in the Communities tab, set its visibility to **Public** during setup. Private groups are invite-only and won't appear in this list.

### Creating a Channel

Tap **New Channel** to launch the channel creation flow. You'll set a name, description, and avatar, then choose whether the channel is public or restricted. Once created, you can post updates, share media, and grow your subscriber base from the channel management screen.

## Community Roles

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Owner" icon="crown">
    The account that created the community. Owners can edit the group name, description, and avatar, promote members to admin, remove members, and delete the community entirely.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="shield-check">
    Admins have moderation powers: they can remove members, pin messages, and help manage day-to-day activity. Owners can grant or revoke admin status from the member list.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Member" icon="user">
    Regular members can send messages in groups and view posts in channels. Members can leave a community at any time from the group's settings screen.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Paid Communities

AfuChat supports exclusive, paid-access communities. When a creator sets an entry price, prospective members must spend the listed amount of **ACoin** (AfuChat's in-app currency) to join. Each paid community card shows the entry price in ACoin before you commit — tap **Join** and AfuChat will confirm the cost and deduct it from your wallet.

If you're a creator, setting an entry price lets you monetize your community and reward members with exclusive posts, early announcements, or members-only discussions. You can also add **tags** to help people discover your community and choose an **emoji** to give it a distinctive identity.

<Note>
  **Communities vs. group chats** — A community group in the Communities tab is publicly discoverable and open for anyone to join (or pay to join if it's a paid community). A group chat created from the Chats tab is private by default: it requires a direct invitation and never appears in the Communities list. Use community groups when you want to grow an audience; use private group chats for close circles and team conversations.
</Note>
