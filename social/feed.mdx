The AfuChat feed is your personal window into the conversations, photos, and ideas shared by the people you follow. Every time someone you follow publishes a post, it surfaces in your **Following** tab so you never miss a moment. When you want to discover content beyond your network, the **For You** tab uses AfuChat's personalization engine to surface relevant posts from across the community.

## Creating a Post

Tap the compose button (pencil icon) in the feed to open the **New Post** screen. You can share:

- **Text** — up to 500 characters, with a live character counter shown at the bottom of the screen.
- **Photo** — tap the **Photo** button in the toolbar to pick an image from your library. The image is cropped to a 4:3 ratio and uploaded automatically when you post. A progress overlay confirms the upload is underway.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the composer">
    Tap the pencil icon in the feed header or the floating compose button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your post">
    Type what's on your mind in the text field. The counter turns red when you approach the 500-character limit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a photo (optional)">
    Tap **Photo** in the bottom toolbar, choose an image from your library, and confirm. A preview appears above the toolbar and can be removed by tapping the × button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Tap the audience pill below your name to choose who can see the post:
    - **Everyone** — visible to all AfuChat users
    - **Followers** — visible only to people who follow you
    - **Only me** — private, visible only to you
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    Tap **Post**. An upload banner appears at the top of the feed while the post is being saved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Posts with photos consistently receive higher engagement than text-only posts. Adding even a single relevant image makes your post stand out in a crowded feed and increases the chance that other users like, comment, and share it.
</Tip>

## Liking, Commenting, and Replying

Each post card shows a row of action buttons at the bottom:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Like" icon="heart">
    Tap the heart icon to like a post. The icon fills red and the count increments instantly. Tap again to remove the like. You can also double-tap a post's image to like it quickly.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Comment" icon="chat-bubble-left">
    Tap the speech bubble icon to open the comment thread for that post. Type your reply and tap **Send**. Replies are threaded so you can respond directly to individual comments.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Share" icon="arrow-up-right">
    Tap the share (forward arrow) icon to send a post to another app or copy the link. Sharing a post generates a public URL at `afuchat.app/p/[id]`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Views" icon="eye">
    The eye icon displays how many times the post has been viewed. This count is read-only and updates in real time.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Following Users

When you follow someone, their public and followers-only posts appear in your **Following** tab. You can follow a user directly from their profile or from any post card — a **Follow** button appears next to the author's name on posts from people you don't yet follow.

Once you follow them the button disappears and their future posts will flow into your feed automatically. To stop seeing someone's posts, visit their profile and tap **Unfollow**.

## Saving Posts

Tap the **bookmark** icon on any post to save it to your collection. Saved posts turn the bookmark icon gold. To review everything you've bookmarked, go to your profile and open **Saved Posts**.

To unsave a post, tap the gold bookmark icon again on the post card or from the Saved Posts screen.

## Reporting Inappropriate Content

If you come across a post that violates AfuChat's community guidelines, tap the **⋮** (three-dot) menu on the post card and choose **Report**. Your report is reviewed by the AfuChat moderation team. You can also choose **Not interested** to hide a specific post from your feed without reporting it, or **Mute @handle** to stop seeing posts from that author across both the For You and Following tabs.

<Warning>
  Repeated abuse of the report function may result in restrictions on your own account. Only report content that genuinely violates community guidelines.
</Warning>
