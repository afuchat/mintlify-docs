Most AfuChat issues can be resolved quickly with a few targeted steps. This guide covers the most common problems users run into, organised by category. If you work through the steps here and still can't resolve the issue, the [Support Center](#contact-support) is always available — our AI assistant responds instantly, and a human agent follows up within 2–4 hours.

## Login & Account Issues

### Can't log in or forgot your password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap 'Forgot Password' on the login screen">
    Enter your registered email address or phone number and tap **Send Reset Link**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox (and spam folder)">
    AfuChat sends a password reset email via our transactional email service. If it isn't in your inbox within 2 minutes, check your spam or junk folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the link in the email">
    The reset link is valid for a limited time. Tap it, set a new password, and log back in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try an alternative sign-in method">
    If you originally signed up with **Google** or **GitHub**, use that OAuth option on the login screen instead of email and password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you've lost access to the email address on your account, open a support ticket categorised as **Account & Login** and provide as much identifying information as possible so the team can verify your identity.
</Tip>

---

### Account locked

If your account has been suspended or locked due to a policy violation or suspicious activity, you will see a notice when you attempt to log in.

- **Policy violation** — Review the notice in-app for the specific reason. You may submit an appeal through the Support Center by opening an **Account & Login** ticket.
- **Suspicious activity** — If AfuChat detected unusual sign-in behaviour, you will be prompted to verify your identity via OTP before regaining access.
- **Two-factor authentication lockout** — If you've lost access to your 2FA device, contact support at **support@afuchat.com** with your account email and @handle.

<Warning>
  Do not create a new account to bypass a suspension. Duplicate accounts may result in a permanent ban. Use the official appeals process instead.
</Warning>

### OTP not received

One-time passwords (OTPs) are sent by SMS or email depending on how your account is configured.

1. **Wait 60 seconds** — OTPs can take up to a minute to arrive, especially during high-traffic periods.
2. **Check your spam folder** — Email-based OTPs sometimes land in junk mail.
3. **Confirm the correct number or email is on file** — If you've changed your phone number or email recently, the OTP is going to the old address.
4. **Tap "Resend"** — Most OTP screens have a resend option. Wait for the cooldown timer before requesting a new code.
5. **Check carrier filters** — Some mobile carriers block shortcode SMS messages. Try requesting the OTP to your email instead.

## Messages & Connectivity

### Messages not sending

AfuChat includes **offline message queuing** — if you're not connected to the internet, your outgoing messages are saved locally and automatically sent the moment your connection is restored. You don't need to retype or resend them manually.

If messages are failing even when you appear to be online:

1. **Check your internet connection** — Switch between Wi-Fi and mobile data to isolate the issue.
2. **Force-close and reopen the app** — This re-establishes the real-time connection to AfuChat servers.
3. **Look for a connectivity indicator** — AfuChat displays an offline banner when it cannot reach the server. Messages sent during this time are queued and will sync automatically on reconnect.
4. **Check if the recipient has blocked you** — Blocked users cannot send messages to the person who blocked them.
5. **Update the app** — An outdated app version may fail to connect properly. Check for updates in the Play Store or refresh your browser tab on the web.

<Note>
  If you were offline and came back online, give the app 10–15 seconds to sync your pending message queue before assuming something is wrong.
</Note>

---

### Real-time updates not loading

AfuChat relies on a persistent live connection for chat, feed updates, and notifications. If updates seem stuck:

1. **Pull to refresh** on the feed or chat screen.
2. **Force-close and reopen the app** to trigger a fresh WebSocket connection.
3. **Check your network** — Realtime connections require a stable internet connection. Spotty Wi-Fi or a weak mobile signal can interrupt the stream.
4. **Disable a VPN or proxy** — Some VPN configurations block WebSocket connections, which AfuChat relies on for real-time features.
5. **Try the web version** at [afuchat.com](https://afuchat.com) to confirm whether the issue is device-specific.

---

### Media not loading

Images, videos, and voice messages are streamed from AfuChat's media servers. If they aren't loading:

1. **Check your internet speed** — Large media files require a stable connection. Try loading a smaller image first.
2. **Clear the app cache** — Go to **Settings → Storage** in the app to clear cached media. This frees up space and forces fresh downloads.
3. **Check available storage** — If your device is nearly full, media may fail to download. Free up at least 500 MB of storage.
4. **Reload the conversation** — Navigate away from the chat and return. This re-triggers media download requests.
5. **Disable data-saving mode** — If you're on a restricted data plan and have enabled data-saving features, large media may be blocked. Check your device network settings.

## Wallet & Payments

### Payment failed

If your top-up or transaction was declined:

1. **Double-check your payment details** — Verify your card number, expiry date, CVV, and billing address match your bank's records.
2. **Confirm sufficient funds** — Ensure your card or M-Pesa wallet has enough balance to cover the amount plus any bank fees.
3. **Check with your bank** — Some banks block international or online transactions by default. Contact your bank to authorise the payment.
4. **Try a different payment method** — If Visa or Mastercard is failing, try M-Pesa, or vice versa.
5. **Wait and retry** — Payment processors occasionally have temporary outages. Wait 10 minutes and try again.

<Note>
  A failed payment should not charge your account. If you see a charge for a transaction that did not complete, contact your bank and open a **Payments & ACoins** support ticket with the transaction reference number.
</Note>

---

### ACoins not credited after top-up

ACoins normally appear in your wallet within **60 seconds** of a successful payment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wait 5 minutes">
    Payment processing can occasionally take a few extra minutes. Refresh your wallet screen by pulling down.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the payment succeeded with your bank">
    Check your bank app or M-Pesa message history to confirm the deduction went through. If no deduction occurred, the payment failed — try again.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collect your transaction reference number">
    Find the reference number in your bank statement, M-Pesa message, or payment confirmation email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a support ticket">
    Go to **Settings → Help → Support Center** and open a **Payments & ACoins** ticket. Include the transaction reference number, the amount, the date and time, and your @handle. The team can manually credit missing ACoins once the payment is verified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

### Transfer didn't go through

If you tried to send ACoins or complete a mini program transaction and it failed:

1. **Check your ACoin balance** — Make sure you had sufficient ACoins at the time of the transfer.
2. **Verify the recipient's @handle** — Transfers to an incorrect handle will fail. Double-check the spelling.
3. **Check for transfer limits** — Some transaction types have daily or per-transaction limits for security reasons.
4. **Look for an error message** — AfuChat shows specific error messages for most failure states. Note the exact wording and include it in your support ticket.

## App Performance

### App is slow or crashing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Restart the app">
    Force-close AfuChat completely and reopen it. This clears any memory leak or stuck process.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restart your device">
    A full device restart clears background processes that may be competing for memory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check available storage">
    Android devices with less than 500 MB of free storage often experience performance degradation. Go to **Settings → Storage** inside AfuChat to clear cached media and offline content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update the app">
    Open the Play Store, search for AfuChat, and install any pending updates. Bug fixes and performance improvements ship regularly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reinstall the app">
    If crashes persist, uninstall AfuChat and reinstall from the Play Store. Your account data is stored server-side and will not be lost.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If the app crashes consistently when performing a specific action (for example, opening a particular chat or loading the feed), note the exact steps and include them when submitting a **Technical Issue** ticket. This helps the team reproduce and fix the bug faster.
</Tip>

---

### Notifications not working

1. **Check in-app notification settings** — Go to **Settings → Notifications** and confirm that the categories you care about (messages, reactions, gifts, etc.) are enabled.
2. **Check device-level permissions** — On Android, go to **Device Settings → Apps → AfuChat → Notifications** and make sure notifications are allowed.
3. **Disable battery optimisation for AfuChat** — Android's battery saver can prevent background notification delivery. Add AfuChat to your device's "unrestricted" or "not optimised" battery list.
4. **Check Do Not Disturb** — If your phone is in Do Not Disturb mode, notifications may be silenced.
5. **Sign out and back in** — This refreshes your notification registration. Go to **Settings → Sign Out**, then sign back in.

---

### Storage usage

AfuChat caches media locally to make loading faster. Over time this can consume significant storage space.

- **Clear media cache** — Go to **Settings → Storage** in the app. You can see a breakdown by category (images, videos, documents) and clear specific buckets.
- **Manage offline videos** — Go to **Settings → Offline Videos** to remove saved video content you no longer need.
- **Automatic cache management** — AfuChat automatically evicts old cache entries when storage is low, but you can manually trigger a clear at any time.
